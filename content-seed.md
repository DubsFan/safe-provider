## Service descriptions

### Supervised Visitation
- What it is: A trained, neutral third-party monitor is present during a parent's time with their child. The monitor observes the visit and can intervene if needed to keep the child safe.
- Who needs it: Families with a court order requiring supervised visitation, or families who agree that supervision is needed for safety.
- What the monitor does: Observes the visit, enforces the rules in the court order, keeps written documentation, and can provide reports to parents and courts.
- Typical duration: 2 hours minimum per visit. Typical visit length is 2-4 hours.
- Where: At a location agreed upon by both parties and the provider.
- Reports: Professional documentation and reports provided to parents and/or courts. Testimony available when requested.
- Card label: "Neutral Supervision"
- Card description: "Calm, structured, child-centered visits led by a trained professional monitor."

### Supervised Exchange
- What it is: A trained, neutral third-party facilitates the handoff of children between parents. Both parents do not need to interact directly.
- Who needs it: Families with a court order requiring supervised exchange, or families who want a neutral third party present during custody transitions.
- What the monitor does: Manages the handoff so both parents arrive and leave at separate times or in a structured sequence. Documents the exchange.
- Typical duration: 15-30 minutes per exchange.
- Where: At a public location agreed upon by both parties and the provider.
- Card label: "Structured Transitions"
- Card description: "Custody handoffs that reduce conflict and protect children during transitions."

### Reports and Court Documentation
- Card label: "Court-Ready Documentation"
- Card description: "Professional reports and testimony for parents and courts."

## FAQ answers (use exactly)

### Cost and pricing

Q: How much does supervised visitation cost?
A: Intake is $50 per person. Supervised visitation is $70 per hour with a 2-hour minimum. A platform scheduling fee of $99 applies to each case booked through SafeProvider. For a typical first visit with two parents and one child, the total is $339. See our pricing page for full details.

Q: How much does supervised exchange cost?
A: Intake is $50 per person. Each supervised exchange is $70. A platform scheduling fee of $99 applies. For a typical first exchange with two parents, the total is $269.

Q: Are there additional fees for more children?
A: SafePair charges a flat $50 per additional child. This is not per hour. Ask during intake for exact pricing for your family.

Q: Is there a sliding scale or low-income option?
A: Yes. SafePair offers sliding-scale rates for qualifying families. Ask about eligibility during intake.

Q: Where does the $99 platform fee go?
A: The $99 SafeProvider platform fee covers intake processing, scheduling coordination, secure payment handling, and case tracking. It is separate from SafePair's provider fees.

### Process

Q: How long does it take to get started?
A: After intake and payment, SafePair reviews your case within 1-2 business days. If accepted, scheduling begins within 3-5 business days. Total: 3-7 business days from intake to first session.

Q: What happens after I pay?
A: Payment reserves your intake slot and triggers case review. SafePair reviews independently. If accepted, they contact you to schedule. If declined, full refund.

Q: Do I need a court order?
A: Not required to start intake. Most families have one. If unsure, consult your attorney.

Q: What documents do I need?
A: Government-issued photo ID for each adult, court order or stipulation (if applicable), other parent's contact information, preferred schedule, any restraining or no-contact orders, names and ages of all children.

Q: What if SafePair declines my case?
A: Full refund of all fees. SafePair reviews every case independently based on professional assessment and capacity.

### Trust and safety

Q: Who is the provider?
A: SafePair, a professional supervised visitation provider based in Oakland, CA. Operates in compliance with California Family Code Section 3200.5 and Standard 5.20, requiring Live Scan background checks and mandated training.

Q: Is this a law firm?
A: No. SafeProvider manages intake, scheduling, and payment only. We do not provide legal advice. Services delivered by SafePair.

Q: What does "neutral third party" mean?
A: Standard 5.20 defines supervised visitation as contact in the presence of a neutral third person. The monitor does not take sides, does not give custody opinions, and follows your court order rules.

Q: Does SafePair handle high-risk or sexual assault cases?
A: Yes. SafePair provides specialized supervision for highly sensitive cases, including sexual abuse and assault matters, under strict professional oversight. Many providers do not accept these cases. SafePair does.

Q: Can I get a refund?
A: If SafePair declines your case, full refund. Once services begin, refunds follow SafePair's cancellation policy.

Q: What turnaround can I tell my client?
